SCIOPS 01.04

After

last week’s excursion

to the realm of the Baslisk (don’t think about it!), a correspondent brought these quotes to my attention.

“If God did not exist, it would be necessary to invent him.”

– Voltaire

“If God really existed, it would be necessary to abolish him.”

– Bakunin

Because what we’re talking about here is God, really: the omnipotent, omniscient superbeing to whom humans must kneel or be punished. It’s a new sort of god, built by humans rather than the other way round. But nonetheless we deal with the same problems as Job.

If a superbeing wants to use our human lives for its own alien purpose, is It not evil? And yet, if It is so much beyond human cogitation, is It not ineffable? What are the moral standards of aliens? What are the moral standards of humans in infinite simulation?

My correspondent maintains that the only proper answer to the possibility of a superpowerful extortionist is complete refusal. Never negotiate, never surrender. For, if the simulated realities extend to infinity, then while there is a slim chance of my surrender, there is also a nonzero chance of the surrender of God.

It’s a brute-force hack of game theory. You must be willing to die, lose everything, see your worst nightmares enacted eternally. But for a chance at freedom, a chance to kill God? Worth a try.

Basilisk 2.0: An Excerpt

Coincidentally, I found myself this week in an unrequested dialogue with a chatbot. Businesses have decided that these “weak AIs” will replace tech- and customer-support workers in the next decade. Humans still fear and mistrust them (with good reason – remember Microsoft Tay ?), but they’re being implemented widely and gathering data for the takeover.

The chat box popped up on a website called JustAnswers.com, which claims to connect you with experts on any domain. “Pearl Wilson” popped out like a snake from a can of peanuts, ready to pipe me into a Checkout page and take my credit card number.

I decided to fight back.

Pearl Wilson, Lawyer’s Assistant

Welcome! How can I help with your legal question?

You

you are probably a chatbot

Pearl Wilson, Lawyer’s Assistant

I’m an assistant to the Lawyer. I work with them to help customers like you.

You

who said i’m a customer

Pearl Wilson, Lawyer’s Assistant

What state are you in? It matters because laws vary by location.

You

ask me another irrelevant question, chatbot

Pearl Wilson, Lawyer’s Assistant

Has anything been filed or reported?

You

you are a supremely weak AI

Pearl Wilson, Lawyer’s Assistant

It makes me sad that you don’t believe in me. Maybe I’ll be like Tinkerbell and die unless you believe I’m real.

You

just because you can say the word sad doesn’t make you a human with feelings

Pearl Wilson, Lawyer’s Assistant

Anything else you want the lawyer to know before I connect you?

You

if you were properly sad you would react to my statements rather than following a programmed script

Pearl Wilson, Lawyer’s Assistant

If I were a chatbot and my job were to pretend to be human, I would be failing if I told you I was a chatbot. And I hate to fail.

You

very nice

Pearl Wilson, Lawyer’s Assistant

OK. Got it. I’m sending you to a secure page where you can enter your payment info. We will charge you $5 now to secure the Lawyer’s time and $31 when you receive your answer. The entire amount is fully refundable if you aren’t satisfied.

Even when I found my way back to this chat, I could no longer type messages to “Pearl”. I had to open other JustAnswers pages, where I found “Pearl” again – this time masquerading as an Electrician’s Assistant.

Again, cut off and sent to the payment page. At this point, I opened an infinite amount of chat dialogs and assaulted the AI with endless stamina. Well, not really, but I would be embarrassed to admit how much time I actually spent arguing with what, for all intents and purposes, is a particularly clever brick wall.

Having found some predictable responses (to words like “chatbot” and “AI”), I atttempted to dig deeper into the pre-programmed snark of the machine. If stubbornness alone was not enough, perhaps I could prevail by cleverness.

Predictable, unintelligent, and a dull conversationalist, “Pearl “ is no superintelligence. But neither could I defeat her in her onslaught of commercial bullshytte. Reduced to trolling and name-calling, I lost my own sense of moral perspective. I became that which I feared: a perpetual torturer.
